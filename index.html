Why it exists

"Volunteer leadership" has never had an honest measure. Most credentials are completion certificates. Volo Index is an evaluation — grounded in two decades of sector practice, not borrowed frameworks.

How it works

Three steps from "I think I'm pretty good" to a number you can stand behind.

01

Sit for a conversation, not a quiz

A 25-minute conversational evaluation probes how you actually lead volunteers, asking real follow-up questions instead of offering multiple choice.

02

Get scored across six dimensions

Your responses are assessed against a research-grounded rubric and placed on a 1–10 developmental scale — deliberately hard to top out.

03

Receive a plan built from your gaps

A personalized development pathway targets the specific competencies where you have the most room to grow next.

The framework

Six dimensions of volunteer-management competency.

Each dimension is anchored in the research literature before a single assessment question is written.

01
Strategy

Strategic Engagement Design

Connects volunteer engagement to mission and strategy — org readiness, where volunteers create genuine value, and the internal case for investing in the function.

02
Recruitment

Recruitment, Matching & Onboarding

Attracts the right volunteers, matches them to roles that fit their motivations and skills, and integrates them in a way that sets up long-term success.

03
Training

Training, Development & Role Support

Prepares volunteers and supports ongoing growth without the leverage of a paycheck — orientation, coaching, and pathways that keep people engaged.

04
Performance

Performance, Impact & Accountability

Manages performance, measures impact, and holds accountability within the unique constraints of an unpaid relationship.

05
Retention

Recognition, Retention & Culture

Sustains commitment through motivation-matched recognition, strong retention practice, and a culture of belonging across staff and volunteers.

06
Ethics

Ethics, Equity & Advocacy

Navigates power dynamics, ensures equitable access, protects data, and advocates for the volunteer function and its resources.

The scale

Four developmental tiers, deliberately hard to top.

Most people land in the middle — and that is exactly the point. The middle is where a development plan has something real to work with.

Foundational
1.0 – 3.0

Basic awareness; instinct-driven; conflates volunteer and employee management.

Developing
3.1 – 5.5

Understands the principles but applies them inconsistently; reactive rather than intentional.

Proficient
5.6 – 7.5

Consistent, intentional practice; can articulate reasoning and adapt to context.

Expert
7.6 – 10.0

Evaluates systems, designs programs, advocates strategically, and mentors others.
